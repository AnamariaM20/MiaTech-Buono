A fashion week is a week-long fashion industry event where fashion designers, brands, or "houses" display their latest collections in runway fashion shows to buyers and the media which influences upcoming fashion trends for the current and approaching seasons

The most prominent fashion weeks are held in the fashion capitals of the world—in chronological order, New York City, London, Milan, and Paris, or the "Big Four".[3] The foundations of fashion week began in Paris in the late 1800s before spreading to New York, Milan, and London in the 20th century. What began as marketing garments in public spaces like racetracks grew into highly publicized events in themselves.[4][5] In the 2000s, themes of sustainability began emerging at fashion weeks and grew popular across the next decade.[6]

Fashion Week, FW - is one of the main events in the fashion world, in which designers, fashion houses and brands demonstrate new collections. It is fashion weeks that set the trends for the current and future seasons. It all started, oddly enough, in Paris, when marketers of different brands dressed women in their new outfits and sent them to public places. For visiting, the places of the greatest gathering of people were chosen - salons, hippodromes, parks. The first fashion show dates back to 1903 and was organized by the New York store Ehrich Brothers. Gradually, the trend for screenings grew throughout the country and abroad. The first fashion week was held in 1943 - it was held in New York. The main goal of that fashion week was to demonstrate an alternative to French clothes, because during the Second World War it was impossible to go to Paris.

Fashion weeks are divided into types: Big Four Fashion Weeks are the most significant, setting trends, Weeks, held 2 times a year - in autumn (usually in September) and spring in 4 cities - New York, London, Milan and Paris. From DRESS Magazin: Haute Couture Week is an exhibition of haute couture organized by the Paris Fashion Syndicate. The name comes from the French haute couture - high sewing, that is, single pieces of clothing, sewn for the most part by hand. Only those fashion houses that are part of the Paris Fashion Syndicate – an association for the most authoritative, significant and successful fashion houses in France – can demonstrate couture outfits as part of Haute Couture Fashion Week. Foreign houses can also get into the Syndicate, but the requirements for them are higher. They are granted the status of corresponding members.
